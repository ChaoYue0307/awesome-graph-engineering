# Launch and sharing kit

Use this copy to introduce the project consistently without overstating the maturity
of the term. The preferred destination is the
[interactive website](https://chaoyue0307.github.io/awesome-graph-engineering/);
technical audiences may prefer the
[GitHub repository](https://github.com/ChaoYue0307/awesome-graph-engineering) or
[Hugging Face dataset](https://huggingface.co/datasets/cy0307/awesome-graph-engineering).

## Fast facts

- **Working idea:** engineer graph-structured AI-agent systems as controllable,
  inspectable wholes.
- **Scope:** roles, topology, typed handoffs, work graphs, state, evidence gates,
  reliability, observability and cost, and evolution.
- **Evidence posture:** 118 curated primary or first-party resources, with publication
  type stated rather than converted into a quality score.
- **Open data:** synchronized JSONL and CSV on GitHub and Hugging Face.
- **Status:** an emerging practitioner term grounded in prior multi-agent, distributed
  systems, workflow, protocol, evaluation, and agent-graph research.

## Suggested post structure

1. Lead with the systems problem, not the project name.
2. State one concrete artifact: the interactive graph, evidence map, or dataset.
3. Include the emerging-term qualification when discussing the definition.
4. Use one destination link and at most two relevant tags, such as
   `#AIAgents` and `#MultiAgentSystems`.
5. Invite a specific action: challenge a boundary, contribute a primary source,
   review a translation, or reuse the dataset.
